


Recruiter Analytics

Recruiter Analytics refers to using data and metrics to evaluate and improve recruiting performance—helping you make smarter hiring decisions, reduce costs, and hire better talent faster.

Here’s a clear breakdown 👇

🔍 What Recruiter Analytics Covers
1. Sourcing Effectiveness
Which channels bring the best candidates (LinkedIn, job boards, referrals, etc.)

Cost per source

Conversion rate per source

👉 Example: If referrals have a 40% hire rate vs. job boards at 10%, you double down on referrals.

2. Pipeline Metrics
Number of candidates at each stage

Drop-off rates (where candidates exit)

Time spent in each stage

👉 Helps identify bottlenecks (e.g., too many candidates stuck in interviews).

3. Time-Based Metrics
Time to Fill – Days from job posting to hire

Time to Hire – Days from candidate application to offer acceptance

👉 Critical for improving speed and candidate experience.

4. Quality of Hire
Performance of new hires

Retention rates (e.g., 90-day or 1-year retention)

Hiring manager satisfaction

👉 This is the most important but hardest to measure metric.

5. Recruiter Productivity
Number of roles handled

Submissions per role

Interviews scheduled

Offers extended and accepted

6. Offer & Conversion Metrics
Offer acceptance rate

Interview-to-offer ratio

Submission-to-interview ratio

👉 Example:

10 submissions → 3 interviews → 1 hire

7. Diversity & Inclusion Metrics
Diversity across pipeline stages

Hiring outcomes by demographic

Bias detection in hiring funnel
